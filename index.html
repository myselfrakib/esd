Context & Scale

The Urban Surge

Today, more than half of the world's population lives in cities. By 2050, that figure is projected to rise to nearly 70%.

Cities are the engines of economic growth, accounting for about 80% of global GDP. However, they also consume over two-thirds of the world's energy and account for more than 70% of global carbon emissions.

4.4B People live in cities today
70% Of global CO2 emissions from cities
60% Projected urban population by 2030
Strategic Pillars

Core Pillars of Sustainable Urbanism

Achieving SDG 11 requires a holistic approach addressing multiple interconnected systems.

Housing & Services

Ensuring access to safe, affordable, and basic housing, plus upgrading slum settlements globally.

Sustainable Mobility

Expanding safe, affordable, and accessible public transit systems with emphasis on active travel.

Urban Resilience

Improving disaster preparedness, mitigating environmental impact, and reducing loss of life.

Circular Resource Flow

Reducing municipal waste, improving air quality, and supporting localized green economies.

Green Mobility

Rethinking Transit & Urban Mobility

Transitioning away from private vehicle dependency toward shared, electric, and active transportation.

  • Integrated Mass Transit: High-capacity rapid transit networks powered by renewable electricity.
  • Active Infrastructure: Protected bike corridors and pedestrianized downtown districts.
  • First/Last Mile Solutions: Scalable micro-mobility schemes and smart ridesharing nodes.

Reduce Air Pollution

Eradicates tailpipe emissions, directly improving local air quality indices.

Reclaim Public Space

Replaces parking lots and highways with parks and public plazas.

Enhance Health & Equity

Encourages active lifestyles and ensures low-cost transport accessibility.

Social Infrastructure

Safe & Affordable Housing

Adequate housing is a fundamental human right. Sustainable cities must accommodate all income levels.

Rapid, unplanned urbanization has led to 1 billion people living in slums and informal settlements today, lacking basic sanitation, clean water, and secure tenure.

"Slum upgrading is not just about brick and mortar; it's about integration, dignity, and economic opportunity."

Key Focus

Participatory Upgrading

Engaging communities in planning upgrades, ensuring they aren't displaced from their social fabrics.

Key Focus

Affordable Building Tech

Utilizing local, low-carbon materials and modular building methods to reduce housing costs.

Key Focus

Policy & Land Rights

Implementing progressive zoning and secure land tenure to prevent sudden displacements.

Disaster Resilience

Building Climate-Resilient Cities

As climate change intensifies weather events, cities must shift from reactive recovery to proactive adaptation.

Sponge Cities

Designing porous urban surfaces (bioswales, green roofs, permeable concrete) to absorb stormwater runoff and recharge groundwater.

Early Warning Systems

Deploying localized sensors and multi-channel notification networks to alert citizens before disaster strikes.

Hardened Infrastructure

Retrofitting seawalls, raising critical utility lines, and designing buildings to withstand seismic and extreme meteorological events.

Circular Systems

Zero Waste & Circular Economies

Transforming waste streams into secondary resource streams to reduce urban footprints.

Traditional cities follow a linear model: "Take, Make, Dispose". Circular cities aim to design waste out of the system entirely, ensuring nutrients and materials flow back into production cycles.

80% Of waste is recyclable or compostable
2.2B Tons of annual municipal waste by 2025
1
Source Separation

Mandating the separation of organics, dry recyclables, and hazardous wastes at source.

2
Localized Processing

Establishing anaerobic digesters for organic waste to produce biogas and fertilizer locally.

3
Upcycling & Repurposing

Creating regional industrial parks where one factory's waste is another's raw material.

Urban Ecology

Reintegrating Nature in Cities

Expanding green cover to improve public health, support biodiversity, and reduce the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect.

  • Micro-Parks & Forests: Converting derelict parcels into high-density native pocket forests using Miyawaki methods.
  • Green Envelopes: Mandating green roofs, vertical gardens, and vegetative facade elements on new developments.
  • Ecological Corridors: Interlinking public parks to allow wildlife movement and migration.

Urban Cooling

Trees and vegetation lower surface temperatures by up to 2°C to 8°C through shading and evapotranspiration.

Mental & Physical Well-being

Access to public green space is proven to lower cortisol levels, reduce cardiovascular risks, and foster social cohesion.

Smart Tech

Smart Infrastructure & Governance

Using technology not just for surveillance, but to optimize resources and elevate civic participation.

Smart Energy Grids

IoT-enabled grids that match power supply to real-time demand, prioritizing local renewable generation.

Responsive Logistics

Real-time traffic management sensors that route transit dynamically and schedule waste pickup only when bins are full.

Inclusive e-Democracy

Mobile portals for participatory budgeting and localized hazard reporting, ensuring citizen voices drive urban action.

The Road Ahead

A Vision for Tomorrow's Cities

Transitioning to sustainable urban habitats requires immediate, collaborative effort across all sectors of society.

Policy Reform

Aligning national urban policies with climate and biodiversity targets.

Capital Mobilization

Leveraging green municipal bonds and global adaptation finance.

Citizen Ownership

Empowering local neighborhoods to build and co-manage micro-projects.
